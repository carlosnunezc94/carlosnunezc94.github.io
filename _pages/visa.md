Unlike the rest of the international students taking graduate courses in the United States, since 2004 Chilean and Singaporean citizens have been eligible for a new type of work visa for non-immigrants, called [H1-B1](https://www.h1b1.com/practices/h-1b1-visa-for-chileans-singaporeans/). 
<p></p>
Although it is necessary to renew it annually, the H1-B1 visa **does not** require the approval of the Non-immigrant Worker Petition by the United States Citizenship and immigration Services. The applicant must apply directly to the US consulate in his or her country by sending the appropriate application and offer letter. Due to the limited availability of H1-B visas, employers will look favorably on applicants who can apply for this visa, since for Chileans there is a stock of **1400 visas of this type annually.**

For more information please visit the following [link](https://www.h1b1.com/practices/h-1b1-visa-for-chileans-singaporeans/).
